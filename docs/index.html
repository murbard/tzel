Elliptic curves

No elliptic curves.
Anywhere.

Every privacy system deployed today relies on elliptic curve cryptography that a sufficiently large quantum computer will break. The "harvest now, decrypt later" threat makes this urgent even before such hardware exists.

TzEL replaces the entire stack: BLAKE2s for hashing, ML-KEM-768 (NIST-standardized lattice KEM) for encryption, hash-based one-time signatures for spend authorization, and STARKs for proofs. The only hardness assumption is the collision resistance of BLAKE2s.

commitment  = BLAKE2s(d, v, rcm, tag)
nullifier   = BLAKE2s(nk, cm, pos)
memo       = ML-KEM-768 + ChaCha20
spend auth = WOTS+ verified in-STARK
proof      = recursive STARK (Cairo + Stwo)

Sign on your hardware wallet.
Prove anywhere.

TzEL separates signing from proving. Even the most lightweight device—a hardware wallet, a phone, a smart card—can authorize a transaction by producing a single hash-based signature. The heavy proof generation is outsourced to any untrusted machine.

The signature is verified inside the STARK circuit. A malicious prover cannot redirect funds—the signature binds to the exact outputs. The prover never sees your signing key and nothing on-chain links back to your address.

Run the prover on your own machine, or delegate to a third-party service running a TEE. In the worst case—a TEE breach—the attacker learns which transaction you made, but still cannot steal funds or forge spends. Proving is a pure computation with no secret key access.

hardware wallet:
  sign(sighash) → wots_signature

untrusted prover:
  witness + signature → STARK proof

on-chain verifier sees:
  proof, nullifiers, commitments
  no keys, no signatures, no links

Your transactions.
Your choice who sees them.

Every transaction is encrypted on-chain by default. No one can read your balances, trace your payments, or link your addresses—unless you decide otherwise.

TzEL gives you fine-grained viewing keys you can share selectively. Give an auditor read access to a single address. Let a portfolio tracker see balances without spending power. Disclose exactly what you choose, to exactly who you choose, and nothing more.

on-chain:      encrypted, unlinkable

detection key: flag incoming txs
viewing key:   read balances + memos
spending key:  full control

you choose who gets what

Built in the open
